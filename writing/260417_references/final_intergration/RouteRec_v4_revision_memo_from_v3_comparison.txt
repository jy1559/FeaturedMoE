RouteRec v4 final revision memo
================================

Goal
----
Use v4 as the base draft, but absorb the strongest clarity/organization gains from v3.
The target is not a hybrid for its own sake. The target is:
1) keep v4's stronger experiment spine,
2) import v3's cleaner method phrasing and appendix organization,
3) remove all draft / placeholder traces.

Bottom line
-----------
Do NOT switch the base to v3 wholesale.
Instead:
- keep v4's main experiment framing, especially Q3 as the three-axis validation question,
- pull selected wording/organization from v3 where it improves clarity without weakening the experiment logic.

Why v4 should remain the base
-----------------------------
1. v4 has the stronger experiment-centered story.
   Its biggest advantage is Q3: it directly validates the three design axes from the method:
   - semantic cue design,
   - stage semantics,
   - router composition.
   This is the closest match to the paper's central promise: the method proposes three axes, and the main text should validate all three in the main experiment section.

2. v4 better preserves the "method -> experiment" contract.
   Reviewer-facing question:
   "Did the paper really test why this structure is necessary?"
   v4 answers this more directly than a stage-only framing.

3. v4 better supports the paper's core novelty claim.
   The claim is not only "staged routing helps."
   The claim is:
   - compact portable cues matter,
   - temporal scope decomposition matters,
   - behavior-guided allocation matters.
   v4's Q3 leaves room for all three in the main text.

What to import from v3
----------------------
The following are genuine strengths of v3 and should be imported into v4.

A. Framework vs. paper-instantiation distinction
------------------------------------------------
v3 is better at separating the general RouteRec design from the fixed paper instantiation.

Action:
- In Method 3.1, adopt v3's stance explicitly:
  "We first describe RouteRec as a design framework and refer to the fixed paper instantiation only when concrete implementation choices matter."
- Keep that distinction visible in 3.1 and 3.2.
- Avoid sounding like every implementation detail is part of the core concept.

Why:
This reduces reviewer resistance to hand-designed implementation choices and makes the paper read as a method paper rather than a one-off engineering report.

B. More precise feature-definition language
-------------------------------------------
v3 is more careful about feature-source wording:
- "optional coarse item-group labels"
- "coarsest item-group proxy available"
This is better than vague "category/theme-like" wording.

Action:
- Replace v4's looser wording in 3.2 with v3-style precise language.
- Keep the definition general enough for cross-dataset portability:
  use "coarsest item-group proxy available in the dataset" as the canonical phrase.
- Then keep v4's portability logic:
  if such a field is unavailable, fall back to a documented missing-signal rule / sequence-derived proxy policy.
- Be explicit once, then stay consistent.

Best combined formulation:
"Focus captures concentration and switching behavior over the coarsest item-group proxy available in the dataset. When such grouping fields are unavailable, the corresponding cues are handled by the fixed missing-signal rule documented in Appendix A (or by documented sequence-derived fallback proxies, if that is truly what the implementation does)."

Important:
Do NOT overclaim fallback behavior if the actual implementation simply omits those cues.
The wording must match code reality.

C. Cleaner z-loss / notation presentation
-----------------------------------------
v3's training-objective phrasing is cleaner and more explicit about using pre-softmax expert logits.

Action:
- In 3.5, revise the z-loss notation using the clearer v3 form.
- Make sure the notation for the logit tensor is explicit and consistent with the rest of the section.
- Remove any notation ambiguity around q / a / router logits.

D. More explicit setup language
-------------------------------
v3 explains the sessionized evaluation unit and held-out cleanup more concretely.

Action:
Use v3-style clarity in 4.1 for:
- why sessions are the evaluation unit,
- what is removed in held-out cleanup,
- why unseen non-target rows are removed,
- why unseen targets are supplementary rather than main-table results,
- why this is not meant to claim direct comparability to canonical raw-sequence leaderboards.

This is one of the places where reviewers will probe hardest.
Make this section direct and procedural, not abstract.

E. Better appendix sectioning
-----------------------------
v3's appendix organization is cleaner and should be adopted in v4.

Recommended appendix structure:
- Appendix A: Method Details and Design Rationale
- Appendix B: Dataset, Evaluation, and Full Results
- Appendix C: Extended Structural Ablations
- Appendix D: Protocol Sensitivity and Supplementary Views
- Appendix E: Objective and Regularization Variants
- Appendix F: Efficiency and Model Cost
- Appendix G: Extended Diagnostics
- Appendix H: Behavior-Slice Analysis
- Appendix I: Extended Transfer Results

Why:
This separation is easier for reviewers to navigate and reduces the impression that the appendix is a dumping ground.

What to keep from v4 without weakening
--------------------------------------
1. Keep Q3 exactly as the three-part design question.
   Do NOT regress Q3 into a narrower "staged structure only" framing.
   The point of Q3 is to validate the three method axes in the main text.

2. Keep the stronger portability narrative.
   The paper's philosophy is not just "we use features."
   It is:
   "we use compact, portable, behavior-grounded cues as a control interface."
   Preserve that line consistently in abstract, intro, method, and Q4.

3. Keep the challenge-to-design-axis logic in the intro/method backbone.
   The intro should continue to move from:
   - shared computation mismatch,
   - temporal-scope mismatch,
   - opaque hidden-state routing,
   to:
   - portable behavioral cues,
   - coarse-to-fine multi-stage routing,
   - behavior-guided expert allocation.

How to revise the main text
---------------------------
1. Introduction
---------------
Keep v4's stronger experimental/problem-driven tone, but import selected v3 clarity.

Recommended edits:
- Preserve the current challenge framing.
- Tighten any sentence that sounds too meta or repetitive.
- One useful v3-style improvement: make the "why routing / why temporal scope / why interpretable control" questions read slightly more directly and less rhetorically inflated.
- Keep the contributions list compact and reviewer-readable.

2. Related Work
---------------
Use the tighter v3-style wording where it improves distinction.
Especially in 2.3:
- emphasize that prior MoE specialization is often latent/task/facet-driven,
- RouteRec instead specializes through observable behavior cues tied to temporal behavioral regimes,
- make the representation-vs-routing role separation explicit.

3. Method
---------
3.1 RouteRec Overview
- Start with the design-framework framing from v3.
- Then transition to the fixed paper instantiation only when needed.

3.2 Portable Behavioral Cues
- Use v3's more precise item-group proxy wording.
- Keep the cue-family taxonomy.
- Be careful that portability claims match implementation reality.
- End the section with one sentence linking cue-family contribution to the experiment section.

3.3 Coarse-to-Fine Multi-Stage Routing
- Keep v4's structural logic.
- Use v3's more concise rationale where it improves readability.
- Explicitly keep the idea that stages differ by evidence scope, routing granularity, and placement.

3.4 Behavior-Guided Expert Allocation
- Keep the v4 framing because it better matches the paper's core novelty.
- But polish with v3's simpler explanatory phrasing:
  first select a family-level control view, then allocate capacity within that view.
- Keep the "semantic prior, not hard assignment" clarification.

3.5 Training Objective
- Adopt v3's cleaner notation and wording.
- Ensure the objective paragraph ends with a direct statement of why no load-balancing term is used in the selected runs:
  not uniform usage for its own sake, but stable behavior-aligned specialization.

How to revise the experiments
-----------------------------
4.1 Experimental Setup
----------------------
This section should become:
- procedural,
- explicit,
- non-defensive,
- auditable.

Must-do edits:
- remove "placeholder" language entirely,
- remove any future-tense "intend to report" language,
- make the sessionized protocol steps explicit,
- state the seen-target view clearly and once,
- clarify held-out cleanup precisely,
- clarify why same processed files / same criterion / same early stopping are used.

Borrow from v3:
- the explanation of session-level split,
- the explanation of unseen non-target row removal,
- the explanation of why seen-target is the main view.

4.2 Q1
-------
v4 structure is fine.
Possible improvement:

- Do not overinterpret mixed results.

4.3 Q2
-------
Keep v4 framing, but add the v3-style reviewer signpost:
"This addresses Challenge 3 and supports the behavior-guided expert-allocation axis."
A short signpost like this helps.

4.4 Q3
-------
Keep v4's three-axis framing. This is the main reason to keep v4 as the base.

Recommended opening:
"Figure 4 directly mirrors the three design axes introduced in the method section."
Then keep:
- panel (a): semantic cue design,
- panel (b): stage semantics,
- panel (c): router composition.

Possible v3-inspired improvement:
After the paragraph, add one clean sentence:

4.5 Q4
-------
Keep v4's portability framing.
This section should remain clearly distinct from Q3:
- Q3 = is the design internally meaningful?
- Q4 = does the method remain useful with limited cue availability?

4.6 Q5
-------

v3's "simple externally computed statistics" wording is useful here.
Be concrete:
- repeat ratio,
- inter-event gap,
- concentration entropy,
- novelty / switching rate.
Then say thresholds are in the appendix.

One thing to improve:
Explicitly say that slices are post hoc diagnostics, not training supervision and not optimized labels.
